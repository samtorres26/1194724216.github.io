My Interests

Sports

Since I was very young I’ve always been heavily involved in sports. I’ve played nearly every sport you could think of ranging from gaelic
football to even golf. However, since the day I was born there has always been the same top few that would play a major role in my life. First of all,
like every Peruvian dad’s son, I inherited soccer as a massive passion of mine. If you ever asked me as a kid what it is that I wanted to
become when i grew up I would’ve said a soccer player like Messi or Maradona. Things changed a bit though, when out of nowhere at the age
of eleven I decided to join rugby because my friends played and Suddenly that became my new dream. It taught me a lot about teamwork and
how everyone seems to have their own role as I was in no way big enough to be anywhere near the scrum without getting crushed. However
because of my quick thinking and wireyness I became a decent winger or full back. Unfortunately eventually I had to choose between
the two once I got old enough to start taking them seriously. Though I was torn, it didn’t surprise many people when I decided to focus
completely on soccer. I am still playing at the moment with UCC and I’m very grateful that this college has such good facilities for
me to continue my hobbies here.

Travel

When I was 13 I moved to San Francisco for a full year as my dad was working there. It was a really cool experience as it is an extremely different
place to live than Ireland, especially when it comes to the weather. I enjoyed my time there in such a massive city but i can’t lie i was happy coming
back to Ireland to all my friends and family I have over here. Another place where I visit a lot more frequently is a small fishing port in Peru named
Chimbote, as that is where my dad is from and where all the other side of my family is from. I love visiting there as the culture is a lot different and
the food there is amazing. However the highlight of my visits in Peru has got to be when I went to Cusco and spent a day hiking to get to Machu Picchu.
The view was absolutely picturesque and is most definitely a moment i will never forget. The other place I have frequently visited over the year is the
beach town of Ardmore on the coast of Waterford. This is due to my grandparents previously having a house down there leading to family getaways with cousins
and things always being down there. It is a place that will also be in my heart due to the many fantastic memories I have of back there.
